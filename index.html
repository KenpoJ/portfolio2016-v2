Introducing... ME!

Earning an Associates degree in Computer Graphics and Multimedia from Utah Career College started my career on the right foot. My first job was designing yellow page ads for directories published across the western United States. Some time afterwards, I returned to school to earn a Bachelors degree in Web Design and Interactive Media from the Art Institute of Salt Lake City. I also attended classes at Southern Utah University studying Illustration, as well as classes at Utah Valley University in their Digital Film program. My education has introduced me to a number of theories, methodologies and software programs that have become invaluable for progressing in my chosen career and taught me that learning is a lifelong pursuit.

As I mentioned, I began my career building ads for yellow pages in InDesign. From there I moved into various positions where my creativity and ability to learn was tested and shaped. Prepress Technician, Graphic Designer, Web Designer, all leading up to my more recent positions that made heavy use of web technologies such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, Bootstrap and AJAX, among others. At Matchbin I worked with small newspapers across the US to build websites, train staff and troubleshoot issues. At the Department of Labor I worked with the content team to maintain the OSHA website; creating new content, building jQuery components to be used on the site and ultimately leading the conversion of the legacy site to a modern, responsive design.

My wife and I are early empty nesters and only recently relocated to the Portland, OR area. We love the abundance of trees, the closeness of the mountains and can be found camping several weekends during the summer months. When I'm not working I read a lot of books, mostly Sci-fi or fantasy and I'm working on my own book. I like to enjoy the outdoors by going on longer motorcycle rides when I can. In fact, I have ridden from Salt Lake City (where we moved from) to Portland twice now! 100 to 150 miles in a trip is not unheard of. And the scenery around here is breathtaking!
